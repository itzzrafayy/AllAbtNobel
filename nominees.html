Nobel Prize Nominations

Recognizing outstanding contributions to humanity since 1901

Nominate a Candidate for the Nobel Prize

The Nobel Prize is awarded annually to individuals and organizations who have made outstanding contributions in the fields of Physics, Chemistry, Physiology or Medicine, Literature, Peace, and Economic Sciences.

Nominations can be submitted by qualified nominators, including university professors, scientists, members of national assemblies and governments, and previous Nobel Prize laureates.

The nomination period is from September to January each year. All nominations are confidential and remain sealed for 50 years.

Nobel Prize in Physics

The most prestigious award in physics, recognizing groundbreaking contributions to our understanding of the universe.

Nomination Criteria

The Nobel Prize in Physics is awarded to those who have made the most outstanding contributions in the field of physics. Nominations should demonstrate:

  • Discovery or invention: A fundamental breakthrough in physical theory or the invention of revolutionary instrumentation
  • Impact: The work should have significantly advanced our understanding of physical phenomena
  • Originality: The contribution should represent a substantial leap rather than incremental progress
  • Verification: The discovery should be experimentally verified when possible
  • Timeliness: While there's no strict time limit, recent work is generally favored

Eligible Fields

The Physics Prize encompasses all branches of physics, including but not limited to:

  • Theoretical physics (quantum mechanics, relativity, particle physics)
  • Experimental physics (condensed matter, nuclear, atomic physics)
  • Astrophysics and cosmology
  • Applied physics with fundamental implications
  • Interdisciplinary work with physics at its core

Nomination Requirements

A complete nomination for the Physics Prize should include:

  • Detailed description of the discovery or invention
  • Evidence of its impact on the field
  • Supporting publications and citations
  • Independent verification when applicable
  • Comparison with previous work in the field

Notable Physics Laureates

Recent winners include:

  • 2022: Alain Aspect, John F. Clauser, and Anton Zeilinger for experiments with entangled photons
  • 2021: Syukuro Manabe, Klaus Hasselmann, and Giorgio Parisi for climate models and complex systems
  • 2020: Roger Penrose, Reinhard Genzel, and Andrea Ghez for black hole discoveries

Nobel Prize in Chemistry

Awarded for groundbreaking discoveries that have expanded our understanding of chemical phenomena and their applications.

Nomination Criteria

The Nobel Prize in Chemistry recognizes exceptional contributions to the chemical sciences. Nominations should demonstrate:

  • Scientific importance: The work should represent a major advance in chemical knowledge
  • Innovation: Development of new theories, methods, or materials
  • Practical applications: Demonstrated or potential benefits to humanity
  • Experimental verification: The work should be reproducible and validated
  • Impact: Significant influence on the field of chemistry

Eligible Fields

The Chemistry Prize covers all areas of chemistry, including:

  • Organic, inorganic, and physical chemistry
  • Biochemistry and molecular biology
  • Materials science and nanotechnology
  • Theoretical and computational chemistry
  • Environmental and green chemistry

Nomination Requirements

A complete nomination for the Chemistry Prize should include:

  • Detailed description of the chemical discovery or innovation
  • Published papers documenting the work
  • Evidence of impact on the field
  • Independent verification of results
  • Statement of the work's significance

Notable Chemistry Laureates

Recent winners include:

  • 2022: Carolyn R. Bertozzi, Morten Meldal, and K. Barry Sharpless for click chemistry
  • 2021: Benjamin List and David W.C. MacMillan for asymmetric organocatalysis
  • 2020: Emmanuelle Charpentier and Jennifer A. Doudna for CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing

Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine

Recognizing discoveries that have revolutionized our understanding of life and improved human health.

Nomination Criteria

The Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine is awarded for discoveries that:

  • Fundamental importance: Transform our understanding of biological processes
  • Medical impact: Lead to significant improvements in human health
  • Scientific rigor: Based on sound experimental evidence
  • Originality: Represent a conceptual or technical breakthrough
  • Verification: Independently confirmed by the scientific community

Eligible Fields

The Medicine Prize encompasses:

  • Molecular biology and genetics
  • Cell biology and physiology
  • Neuroscience and brain research
  • Immunology and vaccine development
  • Disease mechanisms and treatments

Nomination Requirements

A complete nomination for the Medicine Prize should include:

  • Detailed description of the discovery
  • Published research papers documenting the work
  • Evidence of impact on medicine or biology
  • Clinical applications if applicable
  • Letters of support from experts in the field

Nobel Prize in Literature

Awarded to authors who have produced outstanding literary work of idealistic tendency.

Nomination Criteria

The Nobel Prize in Literature is awarded to writers who:

  • Literary excellence: Demonstrate exceptional artistic merit
  • Idealistic tendency: Promote humanistic values
  • Originality: Offer unique perspectives or styles
  • Impact: Influence literature and culture
  • Body of work: Typically awarded for lifetime achievement

Eligible Genres

The Literature Prize recognizes work in all literary forms:

  • Fiction (novels, short stories)
  • Poetry and drama
  • Non-fiction with literary merit
  • Philosophical and historical works
  • Works in all languages

Nomination Requirements

A complete nomination for the Literature Prize should include:

  • Detailed analysis of the author's work
  • Published works (originals or translations)
  • Critical reviews and scholarly analysis
  • Evidence of international recognition
  • Statement of the work's significance

Notable Literature Laureates

Recent winners include:

Nobel Peace Prize

Awarded to those who have done the most or best work for fraternity between nations, disarmament, and peace congresses.

Nomination Criteria

The Nobel Peace Prize recognizes:

  • Peace efforts: Work to prevent conflict or resolve disputes
  • Human rights: Advancement of civil liberties and justice
  • Disarmament: Reduction of military threats
  • Humanitarian work: Assistance to vulnerable populations
  • Impact: Demonstrated results of peacebuilding efforts

Eligible Candidates

The Peace Prize may be awarded to:

  • Individuals or organizations
  • Activists and human rights defenders
  • Diplomats and political leaders
  • Humanitarian workers
  • Journalists and whistleblowers

Nomination Requirements

A complete nomination for the Peace Prize should include:

  • Detailed description of peace work
  • Documentation of achievements
  • Evidence of impact
  • Supporting materials (reports, articles)
  • Letters of recommendation

Notable Peace Laureates

Recent winners include:

  • 2022: Ales Bialiatski, Memorial, and Center for Civil Liberties for human rights work
  • 2021: Maria Ressa and Dmitry Muratov for safeguarding freedom of expression
  • 2020: World Food Programme for combating hunger

Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences

Awarded for outstanding contributions to the science of economics.

Nomination Criteria

The Economics Prize recognizes work that:

  • Theoretical innovation: Advances economic theory
  • Empirical research: Provides new insights through data analysis
  • Methodological contribution: Develops new analytical tools
  • Policy impact: Influences economic policy
  • Scientific rigor: Meets high academic standards

Eligible Fields

The Economics Prize covers all areas of economics:

  • Macroeconomics and monetary theory
  • Microeconomics and game theory
  • Econometrics and statistical methods
  • Behavioral and experimental economics
  • Development and international economics

Nomination Requirements

A complete nomination for the Economics Prize should include:

  • Detailed description of the economic contribution
  • Published research papers
  • Evidence of impact on the field
  • Independent verification of results
  • Letters of support from experts

Notable Economics Laureates

Recent winners include:

  • 2022: Ben S. Bernanke, Douglas W. Diamond, and Philip H. Dybvig for research on banks and financial crises
  • 2021: David Card, Joshua D. Angrist, and Guido W. Imbens for empirical contributions to labor economics
  • 2020: Paul R. Milgrom and Robert B. Wilson for improvements to auction theory
